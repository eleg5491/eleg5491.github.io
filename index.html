The course focuses on the knowledge of deep learning and its applications (mainly) to computer vison. For the general audience, we suggest some good resources below.

Courses:
  • cs231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition. Featuring concrete, fresh and interesting case studies. Highly recommended.
  • cs4476: Computer Vision. Classical computer vision course offered by James Hays.
  • Introduction to Computer Vision. By Noah Snavely at Cornell.
  • 6.869: Advances in Computer Vision. MIT Graduate-level course offered by Antonio Torralba.
  • cs229: Machine Learning. The famous Andrew Ng style course with easy start and good intuitions. There are so many related materials about this course where you can take a peek online.
  • Machine Learning. By Tony Jebara at Comlumbia University.

Textbooks:
  • Deep Learning. By Ian Goodfellow and it covers most necessities.
  • Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications. Most popular among the community, free e-book online and it covers a wide span of topics from low-level image processing to high-level vision recognition.
  • Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning. Machine Learning Bible. Everyone should know at least some of it.

Misc:
  • Arxiv sanity preserver. By Karpathy where the arXiv papers are arranged in a neat way.
  • Top-tier journals and conferences in computer vision. Getting to know the vision community better.
  • The Computer Vision Industry. Summarized by Prof. David Lowe. A rough list of companies related to computer vision and robotics.
  • The Annual Consumer Electronics Show. The biggest, longest, most famous AI-based convention and exhibition where research work of the academia are transferred into the industry.
